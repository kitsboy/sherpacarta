---

## Brand Voice & Tone

**Voice:** Principled. Calm. Uncompromising. Hopeful but urgent. Intellectual without being academic. Human.

**Never:**
- Corporate jargon ("leverage", "synergy", "disrupt")
- Fear-mongering without solutions
- Partisan language
- "Web3" or crypto-bro vibes (even though we accept Bitcoin)

**Always:**
- Ground claims in history (Magna Carta, UDHR, Iceland 2011)
- Center the individual human being
- Emphasize "living document" and expansion of rights
- Acknowledge that this starts as moral force, not yet legal force
- Credit volunteers and on-chain transparency

**Signature phrases:**
- "Privacy is not a feature. It is a birthright."
- "Rights begin as declarations."
- "Commerce serves humanity. Humanity does not serve commerce." (Art. 114)
- "The rights we fail to assert today become the tyrannies our children inherit tomorrow."

## Common Objections & Rebuttals

**"This isn't legally binding."**
"Neither was the Magna Carta for the first 50+ years. Neither was the UDHR when it was signed. Declarations create the moral and political space in which law later grows. That is the point."

**"Another rights document won't change anything."**
"Every major rights expansion in history started as words on paper with no immediate enforcement. The enforcement came later because people organized around the words. SherpaCarta is the organizing document for the next phase."

**"Platforms will just ignore it."**
"Many will. The ones that don't will gain moral and eventually regulatory advantage. The ones that do ignore it will face growing public, legal, and employee pressure. We are building the standard they will be measured against."

**"Why Bitcoin?"**
"Because we refuse every other form of capture. No government grants. No corporate sponsorship. No VC. Bitcoin is the only widely usable, censorship-resistant, transparent funding mechanism that matches our principles."

## Metrics That Matter

- Signatory count (local + published)
- Countries represented
- Languages with at least one full translation + legal review
- Number of organizations that have formally endorsed
- GitHub stars / forks (when public)
- On-chain donation volume + transparency reports
- Press mentions + quality of coverage
- Amendment proposals submitted

Vanity metrics (pageviews, social followers) are secondary to adoption and signal of seriousness.
