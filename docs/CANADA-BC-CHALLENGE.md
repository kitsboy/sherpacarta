# Canada & British Columbia Challenge

**SherpaCarta as Model Law for Digital Rights**  
**Version 1.0 — 2026-07-02**

---

## Why Canada First

Canada has strong privacy foundations (PIPEDA, provincial equivalents) but gaps remain on **algorithmic accountability**, **data sovereignty**, and **platform power**. British Columbia is an ideal pilot:

- Active privacy commissioner culture
- Tech sector concentration (Vancouver, Victoria)
- Progressive municipal governance open to civic charters
- Bilingual obligation mirrors our international i18n roadmap
- Federal–provincial split lets us test **provincial adoption → federal harmonization**

---

## The Ask

Adopt SherpaCarta's 114 articles — or a BC-tailored subset — as:

1. **Model legislation** for a Digital Rights Act (BC)
2. **Federal reference framework** for PIPEDA modernization
3. **Municipal Safe Harbour** standard for city digital services
4. **Procurement requirement** for vendors serving BC government

## Proof & Legitimacy Stack

Every official submission version should be:

1. **Hashed** (SHA-256 of canonical text)
2. **Timestamped** via [Satohash](https://satohash.giveabit.io) / OpenTimestamps on Bitcoin
3. **Signed** by organizations via Nostr or formal letter
4. **Versioned** in git with BUILD numbers

This gives policymakers court-admissible proof of what was presented and when.
