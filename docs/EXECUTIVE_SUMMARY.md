# SherpaCarta — Executive Summary

**The Global Digital Magna Carta for the 21st Century**  
**Version 2.0 — 2026**

---

## The Problem

The digital revolution has created unprecedented powers of surveillance, control, and behavioral manipulation by both states and private corporations whose market capitalizations exceed the GDP of most nations.

Existing frameworks (GDPR, national laws, the original Magna Carta tradition) are:

- Geographically limited
- Outdated for AI, algorithmic decision-making, and surveillance capitalism
- Not binding on the dominant private platforms
- Fragmented and unenforceable across borders

**8 billion people lack a unified, living declaration of digital human rights.**

---

## The Solution: SherpaCarta v2.0

SherpaCarta is a **living, globally-signed charter** of 114 articles protecting privacy, data sovereignty, freedom of expression, and algorithmic rights for every human on Earth.

It synthesizes:

- The 1215 Magna Carta (rule of law over arbitrary power)
- The 1948 Universal Declaration of Human Rights
- The 2011 Icelandic Constitutional Bill (first crowdsourced constitution)
- Modern realities of AI, surveillance capitalism, and platform power

**Key innovations in v2.0:**
- Explicitly binds corporations and algorithms, not just states
- "Privacy by Design" as mandatory default (Art. 11)
- Full data sovereignty + right to be forgotten with teeth (Art. 12, 47)
- Right to algorithmic transparency and protection from algorithmic discrimination (Art. 61–62)
- Internet access as a fundamental utility right (Art. 22)
- Living document clause: rights may only expand, never contract (Art. 114)

## Strategic Positioning

SherpaCarta is **not**:

- A treaty or binding legislation (yet)
- A corporate or government initiative
- Another compliance checkbox

SherpaCarta **is**:

- A moral and political declaration that creates precedent
- A tool for activists, legislators, journalists, and citizens
- A living document that can evolve into enforceable norms and law
- The spiritual and practical successor to the Magna Carta for the digital age

**Tagline:**  
"Privacy is not a feature. It is a birthright."
